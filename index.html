Monsoon Mission Project: ISMR Prediction with CFSv2

Overview

This project focused on developing a dynamical system for seasonal prediction of Indian Summer Monsoon Rainfall (ISMR) using the NCEP Climate Forecast System version 2 (CFSv2). The work explored the potential predictability of ISMR by analyzing the impacts of sea surface temperature (SST) and soil moisture, leveraging advanced statistical techniques.

Technologies Used

  • Fortran 90, Python, Matlab
  • CFSv2, NCO, CDO
  • Data: CMIP5, NCEP CFSR

Methodology

Utilized CFSv2 retrospective forecasts at five lead times to assess ISMR predictability. Applied ANOVA, relative entropy, and mutual information to quantify the sensitivity of ISMR to initial conditions. Analyzed SST and soil moisture impacts on monsoon variability, focusing on teleconnections with ENSO and IOD.

Results

Improved ISMR prediction skill by 17% in phase and 20% in amplitude compared to CFSv1, as published in multiple journals. Reduced dry bias over Indian landmass by addressing SST biases in the tropical Indian Ocean.
